On the moonbook.

1. So begins this allworld, when the while of now begins.

1.1. The now is the eversmall while of time untold.
1.2. The blink is the while of untold nows.
1.3. The tick is the while of sixty blinks.

1.4. The mark is the while of sixty ticks, or sixty sixty blinks.

1.5. The stound is the while of sixty marks, or sixty sixty ticks.
1.5.1. The day is the while of ten stounds.

1.6. The week is the while of six days, or sixty stounds, or sixty sixty marks.
1.6.1. The month is the while of five weeks.
1.6.2. The tide is the while of three months.
1.6.3. The seel is the while of twain tides.

1.7. The year is the while of twain seels, or sixty weeks, or sixty sixty stounds.
1.7.1. The wheel is the while of twain years.
1.7.2. The axle is the while of twain wheels.

1.8. The wain is the while of fifteen axles, or sixty years, or sixty sixty weeks.
1.9. The forever is the while of telling wains, or the evergreat while of time atelling.

1.10. So ends this allworld, when the while of forever ends.

2.1. The axle is the while of the mouthborn wheel and the arseborn wheel.
2.1.1. The mouthborn wheel is the while of the spokeskin year and the ringworm year.
2.1.2. The arseborn wheel is the while of the spikeskin year and the ridgeworm year.

2.2. The year is the while of the outer seel of shell and the inner seel of bone.
2.2.1. The outer seel of shell is the while of the tide of spring and the tide of summer.
2.2.1.1. The tide of spring is the while of the months of crankshell, darkshell, and whelkshell.
2.2.1.2. The tide of summer is the while of the months of sparkshell, foxbone, and furtbone.
2.2.2. The inner seel of bone is the while of the tide of fall and the tide of winter.
2.2.2.1. The tide of fall is the while of the months of falkbone, eftbone, and frogbone.
2.2.2.2. The tide of winter is the while of the months of fishbone, lurkshell, and jerkshell.

2.3. The month is the while of the unmoon, the waxing moon, the fullmoon, and the waning moon.
2.3.1. The waxing moon is the while of the bentmoon, the halfmoon, and the billowmoon.
2.3.2. The waning moon is the while of the billowmoon, the halfmoon, and the bentmoon.
2.3.3. The waxing moon spans the first three weeks of the month.
2.3.4. The full moon spans the third week of the month.
2.3.5. The waning moon spans the last three weeks of the month.
